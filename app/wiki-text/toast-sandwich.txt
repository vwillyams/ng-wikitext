A **toast sandwich** is a [[sandwich]] made by putting a thin slice of [[toast]] between two thin slices of bread with a layer of [[butter]], and adding salt and pepper to taste. Its origins can be traced to the [[Victorian era|Victorian]] years. A recipe for making it is included in the 1861 [[Mrs Beeton's Book of Household Management|Book of Household Management]] by [[Isabella Beeton]].<ref name="BBC News Magazine">{{Cite web|last=Lane|first=Megan|title=The toast sandwich and other hyper-cheap meals|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/magazine-15760897|work=BBC News Magazine |date=17 November 2011}}</ref>

##Nutritional information##
According to the [[Royal Society of Chemistry]], the average toast sandwich contains 330 [[Calorie]]s.<ref name="BBC News"/>

##Public recognition##
<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:Toast sandwich - Public reception.jpg|thumb|right|The [[Royal Society of Chemistry]] offers toast sandwiches to the British public.]] -->
The toast sandwich was named by the scientists of the [[Royal Society of Chemistry]] "Britain's cheapest lunchtime meal" in November 2011, with the cost cited as being as low as 7.5p per sandwich.<ref name="BBC News"/> The Royal Society of Chemistry is offering [[Pound sterling|£]]200 to whosoever can create a cheaper alternative.<ref name="BBC News"/><ref name="The Guardian"/><ref name="Daily Mail">{{cite web|author=Fiona Macrae|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2062073/Toast-sandwich-Short-cash-Have-austerity-lunch--toast-2-slices-bread.html|title=Short of cash for lunch? How about an austerity sandwich... that's toast between two slices of bread|publisher=[[Daily Mail]]|date=17 November 2011|accessdate=2014-05-30}}</ref>

Dr. [[John Emsley]] claims that this sandwich will lead Britons out of "harsh economic times."<ref name="Daily Mail"/>

### Menu item ###
[[File:Two toast sandwiches, the Fat Duck, November 2012.jpg|thumb|right|The toast sandwich served as a [[side dish]] at [[celebrity chef]] [[Heston Blumenthal]]'s restaurant [[The Fat Duck]]]]
The toast sandwich is featured as a [[side dish]] for the [[main course]] item the "Mad Hatter's Tea Party (c.1892)"-an *[[Alice in Wonderland]]* inspired dish served alongside a [[mock turtle soup]] where a [[fob watch]] formed of freeze-dried beef stock covered with [[gold leaf]] is dropped into a tea cup and has a beef stock "tea" poured over it<!--at table?--> that dissolves the gold and the watch-on the [[tasting menu]] of [[celebrity chef]] [[Heston Blumenthal]]'s restaurant [[The Fat Duck]].<ref name="News.com.au">{{cite web|author=Dan Stock|url=http://www.news.com.au/lifestyle/food/the-fat-duck-in-melbourne-heston-blumenthal-has-ballot-system-for-bookings/story-fn93ypt9-1227061606696|title=The Fat Duck in Melbourne: Heston Blumenthal has ballot system for bookings|publisher=[[News.com.au]]|date=17 September 2014|accessdate=2014-10-08}}</ref><ref name="Herald Sun">{{cite web|author=Aaron Langmaid|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/news/fat-chance-youll-get-a-table-at-heston-blumenthals-fat-duck-restaurant-at-crown-in-melbourne/story-fni0fiyv-1226870004830?nk=e4c54a27fd7413d6387dd2e2cb5aaeec|title=Fat chance you'll get a table at Heston Blumenthal's Fat Duck restaurant at Crown in Melbourne|publisher=[[Herald Sun]]|date=31 March 2014|accessdate=2014-10-08}}</ref>

"Mad Hatter's Tea Party (c. 1892)" contains 12 toast sandwiches.<ref name="KCRW">{{cite web|author=Sarah Rogozen|url=http://blogs.kcrw.com/goodfood/2013/12/heston-blumenthal-on-mock-turtle-soup/|title=Heston Blumenthal on Recreating Lewis Carroll's Mock Turtle Soup|publisher=[[KCRW]]|date=31 December 2013|accessdate=2014-10-08}}</ref> According to American [[public radio]] station [[KCRW]], Blumenthal's recipe for the toast sandwich involves [[Bone marrow (food)|bone marrow]] salad, [[egg yolk]] [[mustard (condiment)|mustard]], [[gastrique]], [[mayonnaise]], and [[Ketchup#Tomato ketchup|tomato ketchup]].<ref name="KCRW"/>

The meal is priced £220 at *The Fat Duck* located in [[Bray, Berkshire|Bray]], [[Berkshire]], [[England]],<ref name="The Fat Duck Tasting Menu">{{cite web|author=|url=http://www.thefatduck.co.uk/The-Menus/Tasting-Menu/|title=The Fat Duck Tasting Menu|publisher=[[The Fat Duck]]|date=|accessdate=2014-10-08}}</ref> while its [[Melbourne]], [[Victoria (Australia)|Victoria]], [[Australia]] location prices it at between ~[[Australian dollar|$]]351<ref name="Herald Sun"/> and $525.<ref name="News.com.au"/>
